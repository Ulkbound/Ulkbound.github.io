At times, I find myself wishing not to be seen. Not to disappear, but to exist just beyond the reach of notice. To find some safety in obscurity, where the pressure of being perceived loosens its grip. The world feels less insistent there. It is a place to rest and, in time, become curious again.

Ulk, for me, grants that space, through small steps and slight movements. It's a way of engaging with the subtle, which, when nourished, unfolds into discovery. I hope that, once out in the open, these songs retain their ability to draw attention to the smaller details and the ways in which they connect.

That is all, for now. Take care, and dwell beyond measure.
